A History of Human-Canine Relations

DOGS: Canis lupus familiaris. Manâ€™s best friend. The dog-human bond has existed for 10-15 thousand years, from the beginning, where man recognized dogs to be very much like themselves-pack animals and hunters.

Imagine our primitive ancestors with a taste for meat gathered together hunting down an antelope - maybe a dog watching on the sidelines - or coming forward at the scent of meat roasting over a fire. Dogs are fiercely loyal and trusting and develop deep bonds with humans. Throwing a dog a few scraps would surely be a sign of invitation to join the human pack.

Once the bond of trust was established, there was nothing the eager and willing dog would not do for its human companion. Humans found they could put dogs to use. Originally wolf-like, humans bred dogs for traits and characteristics best suited for their needs.

  • Dogs with dense coats could be utilized in cold climates.
  • Dogs with the keenest smell would become trackers.
  • Small dogs with short legs could pull animals from burrows.
  • Sleek, long-legged dogs could be made to race.
  • And many more such breeds developed for herding, fighting, etc.

Each breed, even each dog - have their own nuances and distinct personalities that are not explored in mere fact books. Dr. Patrona's Puppy Paradise hopes to provide readers with the myriad information that Dr. Patrona accumulated in her storied career, so that the reader may be better informed with regards to their canine companions.
